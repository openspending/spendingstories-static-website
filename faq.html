Frequently Asked Questions

Why did you build this?

News stories are full of amounts. “Huge costs” can promote a story from the front page or relegate it to a corner on page 19.

In the OpenSpending project, facilitated by The Open Knowledge Foundation, we wanted to create a tool to help citizens and journalists to compare and make sense the costs floating in the space. With Spending Stories we wish to make it easier to compare amount and for you to add your own.

What is a top story?

Spending Stories categorizes its stories by their relative impact and the likelihood that the amounts of money they feature will resonate with the user. “Top stories” are widely read stories from major newspapers and tabloids, whereas “other stories” are less well known.

How does the cards visualization choose stories for a given amount?

Whereas the scale visualization shows how an amount fits into the whole landscape of amounts in the Spending Stories database, the cards visualization picks out stories featuring amounts that are intuitively comparable to the amount (e.g. twice or half as much is “comparable” while 1/256 is not).

Where does the data for Spending Stories come from?

The data comes from various places. For the initial data we have mostly focussed on finding spending stories in news articles and tabloids since these are stories people know of, but we also get data from OpenSpending. Now the data comes from you or other people like you, who contribute stories via the contribution wizard.

If you’re interested in a particular story you can see where it came from by clicking on the Source button. That will take you to the original news article or place where the data is available. We require all contributors to identify the source of the spending story.

How do I embed a story?

Stories can be embedded using the Share button which appears on both the scale and cards visualizations. Pressing this button reveals a snippet of HTML which can be pasted into a website to embed the story.

How do I add my own spending story?

To start contributing a new story to the database, press the Contribute button, located to the right of the Compare button at the top of the interface. This will take you to the story contribution wizard, which will present you with steps you can follow to add a new story.

Is it open source?

Yes! The source code for Spending Stories is hosted on GitHub. We hope the community will help build on the project by adding new features and fixing bugs.

Can I create my own site with the Spending Stories code?

Please do! We would love to see new Spending Stories sites springing up, particularly if they deal with currencies and geographical areas not covered in the original release.

If enough users create localized instances of Spending Stories, exciting interactions between the sites will become possible (e.g. comparing amounts across different countries).

Can I create it in my own language?

Yes! Besides Spending Stories instances with new countries and currencies, we’re hoping to see the community translate Spending Stories into new languages.

How do I contribute to the code?

Visit the Spending Stories GitHub repo and create a fork of the code. If you make changes that you think should become part of the main branch, create a pull request .
